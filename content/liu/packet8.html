binomial random variables

requirements for a variable to have a binomial dist.

  • binary: success or failure
  • independent: events don't effect each other
  • fixed # of trials
  • some chance of success for each trial

all have to be satisfied for it to be a binomial dist.

binomial formula

\[P(X=k)=\binom{n}{k} \cdot p^k \cdot (1-p)^{n-k}\]
  • n = # of trials
  • k = # of successes
  • p = chance of success for a single trial
  • 1-p = chance of failure for a single trial
  • n-k = # of failures
