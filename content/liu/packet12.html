estimating a population mean - intro

so recall that the std dev of a sampling distr of a population for the mean was \(\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}\)
yeah to get the CI of a mean we do this: \[\bar{x} \pm t^* \cdot \frac{s_x}{\sqrt{n}}\]

to find \(t^*\) we do \(\text{invT}(area=\text{left tail area}, df=n-1)\)

conditions for this CI

  • rng (sample or assignment)
  • 10% (doesn't apply for assignment)
  • normal/large sample- if ANY of these conditions are satisfied you're golden
    • population distr is ~normal
    • \(n \geq 30\)
    • sample data doesn't have skews/outliers to infer the first condition
