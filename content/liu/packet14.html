chi-square test for goodness of fit

this test is used to compare categorical distributions and see how likely it is you got one by sampling from the other

hypotheses

\(H_0\): the claimed distr [ctx] is true
\(H_a\): the claimed distr [ctx] isn't true

conditions

  • rng
  • 10%: sum of all instances (in the harvard wksht it was 2023 total admits)
  • large counts: expected counts of all categories is at least 5!! not 10, 5!!!

formula

\[\chi^2=\sum \frac{(O-E)^2}{E}\]

here, O is the observed value & E is the expected value for each category
then use chi squared cdf on your calculator w/ df = (# of categories) - 1 to get the final p-value

chi-square test for independence

hypotheses

\(H_0\): there isn't an assoc. between var 1 & var 2 \(H_a\): there is one

what the fuck is the difference between all these tests

  • gof: 1 sample, 1 var, testing if a claimed distribution actually works
  • homo: 2+ samples, 1 var, testing if being in a diff sample makes any diff
  • indep: 1 sample, 2 vars, testing if across the same sample shit's related
