what's a sampling distribution? day 1

statistic: a val from a sample (\(\bar{x}\), \(\hat{p}\), \(s\))

parameter: a val that describes a population (\(\mu\), \(p\), \(\sigma\))

sampling distr: distr of a stat from all possible samples of the same size from the same population
a stat's an unbiased estimator if the mean of the sampling distribution is equal to the parameter

the sampling distribution of \(\hat{p}\)

\[\mu_{\hat{p}}=p\] \[\sigma_{\hat{p}}=\sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}}\] if the 10% condition is met i guess

large counts condition: basically the same as it is for binomial distrs, if \(np \geq 10\) and \(n(1-p) \geq 10\), the sampling distribution is approximately normal
and if it's approximately normal, \[z=\frac{\hat{p}-p}{\sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{n}}}\]

sampling distribution of difference between 2 proportions (or just variables)

  • shape: approx. normal if
    • \(n_1 \cdot p_1 \geq 10\)
    • \(n_1(1-p_1) \geq 10\)
    • \(n_2 \cdot p_2 \geq 10\)
    • \(n_2 (1-p_2) \geq 10\)
  • center: \(\mu_{\hat{p_1}-\hat{p_2}}=p_1-p_2\)
  • variability: \(\sigma_{\hat{p_1}-\hat{p_2}}=\sqrt{\frac{p_1(1-p_1)}{n_1}+\frac{p_2(1-p_2)}{n_2}}\)

evaluating a claim

  1. assume the null hypothesis- "it's probably nothing"
  2. do a simulated sampling distribution given said hypothesis
  3. get the probability that the observed result happened
  4. if it's strictly less than 5%, it's probably something, not nothing
